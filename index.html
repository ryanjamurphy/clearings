Tap any night to see the hour-by-hour breakdown, moon, and darkness window.

A viewing window runs from nautical dusk (stars appearing) for ~3 hours. Prime needs full astronomical darkness with the moon down or nearly new. Around the June solstice at this latitude the sky never gets fully dark — the tool will say so.
Days 1–3 are reliable; 4–7 are an outlook that firms up as they approach — check back each morning. Weather from Open-Meteo; sun & moon computed on-device. Spots and thresholds are approximate and easy to change — just ask.
